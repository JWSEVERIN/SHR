About Us

Sunflower Highland Ranch offers a unique experience centered around Highland cows and sunflowers, blending rustic charm with natural beauty. While the ranch is known for its scenic trails and equestrian activities, it also features Highland cows as part of its pastoral landscape. These shaggy, gentle animals are often seen grazing among sunflowers, creating a picturesque setting that appeals to families and nature enthusiasts alike. The ranch provides guided tours and interactive experiences where visitors can learn about the Highland breed, their care, and their role in sustainable farming practices.

For those interested in a deeper connection with Highland cows, Sunflower Highland Ranch offers opportunities to participate in farm activities, including feeding and petting the animals, as well as educational sessions on their heritage and behavior. The ranch also hosts seasonal events, such as farm-to-table dinners and farm festivals, which highlight the importance of preserving rare breeds like the Highland cow.

In addition to its on-farm experiences, Sunflower Highland Ranch supports conservation efforts and promotes responsible breeding practices. The ranch is committed to maintaining a healthy, sustainable environment for its animals and the surrounding ecosystem. Visitors can explore the ranch’s history, including its role in preserving the Highland breed, and learn about the challenges and rewards of raising these unique cattle.

The Highland

Highland cattle (Bos taurus), (Scottish Gaelic: Bò Ghàidhealach), also known as Heilan' coo, are a hardy, rustic breed of domestic cattle originating from the Scottish Highlands and Western Islands. They are distinguished by their long, shaggy double coats and sweeping horns, which allow them to withstand the region's cold, wet, and windy climate.

  • Physical Traits: Mature bulls average 650 kg (1,433 lbs) and stand about 125 cm (4 ft 3 in) tall, while females average 450 kg (992 lbs) and stand 105 cm (3 ft 8 in). Their coats come in several colors, with reddish-brown being the most common (approx. 60%), followed by yellow, black, dun, and brindle.
  • Primary Uses: The breed is reared primarily for beef, which is known for being lean, well-marbled, and low in cholesterol. Historically, they also served as draught animals for plowing and provided milk for remote communities.
  • Behavior & Lifespan: A group of Highland cattle is called a "fold." They possess a clear social hierarchy, are excellent mothers with low calving difficulties, and have a notably long lifespan of 15–20 years.
